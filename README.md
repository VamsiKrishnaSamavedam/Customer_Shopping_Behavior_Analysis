# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer shopping behavior using transactional data from 3,900 purchases across multiple product categories. The objective is to uncover meaningful insights into customer spending patterns, subscription behavior, product preferences, customer segments, and revenue trends to support better business decisions.

## Dataset Summary
- **Total Rows:** 3,900
- **Total Columns:** 18
- **Key Features:**
  - Customer demographics: Age, Gender, Location, Subscription Status
  - Purchase details: Item Purchased, Category, Purchase Amount, Season, Size, Color
  - Shopping behavior: Discount Applied, Previous Purchases, Frequency of Purchases, Review Rating, Shipping Type
- **Missing Values:** 37 missing values in the `review_rating` column

## Tools & Technologies
- **Python** – Data cleaning, preprocessing, and feature engineering
- **Pandas** – Data handling and transformation
- **PostgreSQL** – SQL-based business analysis
- **SQLAlchemy** – Database connection and data loading
- **Power BI** – Interactive dashboard and visual storytelling

## Data Preparation and Cleaning
The dataset was first processed in Python before loading it into PostgreSQL for analysis.

### Steps performed:
- Loaded the dataset using Pandas
- Performed initial exploration using `info()` and `describe()`
- Checked for null values
- Imputed missing values in `review_rating` using the median rating of each product category
- Renamed columns to snake_case for consistency and readability
- Created new features such as:
  - `age_group`
  - `purchase_frequency_days`
- Verified redundancy between `discount_applied` and `promo_code_used`
- Dropped redundant columns where needed
- Loaded the cleaned dataset into PostgreSQL for SQL analysis

## SQL Business Analysis
Using PostgreSQL, the project answered several important business questions:

1. Revenue generated by male vs. female customers  
2. High-spending customers who used discounts but still spent above average  
3. Top 5 products with the highest average ratings  
4. Comparison of average purchase amount by shipping type  
5. Subscribers vs. non-subscribers in terms of customer count, average spend, and total revenue  
6. Products with the highest percentage of discounted purchases  
7. Customer segmentation into New, Returning, and Loyal groups  
8. Top 3 most purchased products in each category  
9. Relationship between repeat buyers and subscription status  
10. Revenue contribution by age group  

## Key Insights
- Male customers generated more total revenue than female customers
- Non-subscribers represented the majority of customers and total revenue
- Express shipping users had a slightly higher average purchase amount than standard shipping users
- Certain products showed a high dependency on discounts
- Most customers fell into the **Loyal** segment based on purchase history
- Young Adult and Middle-aged groups contributed strongly to total revenue

## Business Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

- **Boost Subscriptions:** Offer attractive benefits to increase subscriber count
- **Strengthen Loyalty Programs:** Reward repeat buyers to retain high-value customers
- **Review Discount Strategy:** Balance promotional activity with profit margins
- **Promote Top Products:** Highlight top-rated and best-selling items in marketing campaigns
- **Target High-Value Segments:** Focus on high-revenue age groups and express-shipping users
